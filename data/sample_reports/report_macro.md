# Macro Economic Outlook — Q1 2025
**Date:** 2025-03-05
**Analyst:** Michael Chen
**Sector:** Macro

## Executive Summary
The US economy continues to demonstrate resilience with above-trend growth and a remarkably tight labor market. However, inflation remains stubbornly above the Fed's 2% target, and geopolitical risks (Middle East tensions, US-China trade friction) pose downside threats. We expect the first rate cut in June 2025, with 50-75bps of total cuts by year-end.

## GDP Growth
Real GDP expanded at 2.8% quarter-over-quarter annualized, driven by robust consumer spending and accelerating business investment in AI infrastructure. Government spending and net exports were modest drags. We expect growth to moderate to 2.0-2.3% in H2 2025 as fiscal stimulus fades and high rates continue to weigh on housing and rate-sensitive sectors.

## Inflation
CPI at 3.1% and core CPI at 3.4% remain above the Fed's target. The disinflationary trend has stalled in early 2025 as services inflation (shelter, healthcare, auto insurance) remains sticky. PCE at 2.6% and core PCE at 2.8% are closer to target but progress has slowed. Tariff policy is an upside risk to the inflation outlook.

## Federal Reserve
The Fed held rates at 5.25-5.50% at the March meeting. The dot plot indicates two 25bps cuts in 2025, though market pricing implies three cuts beginning in June. Balance sheet runoff (QT) continues at $60B/month in Treasuries and $35B/month in MBS. Risk: if tariff-induced inflation spikes, the Fed could delay or reduce cuts.

## Labor Market
The labor market remains remarkably tight with 3.8% unemployment and 275K average monthly job gains. Wage growth at 4.1% YoY is gradually decelerating but remains above the level consistent with 2% inflation. The tech sector continues to experience layoffs, but these workers are being readily absorbed by other industries.

## Key Risks
1. **Tariff Escalation:** New tariffs could re-ignite inflation and disrupt supply chains
2. **Geopolitical:** Middle East conflict escalation could push oil above $100/bbl
3. **Debt Ceiling:** Political brinkmanship around debt ceiling could cause market volatility
4. **Commercial Real Estate:** $1.5T in CRE debt maturing through 2027; office distress is rising
